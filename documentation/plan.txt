

Home:
- This is a personal view of tasks / events assigned to you.
- Day/Week/Month filters.
- Quick actions for Complete, Edit, Delete (if you made the task).

Tasks: (This is a Creation or Edit Screen)
- Tasks are chores, events, appointments, sports, school activities, reminders.
- Adults can create and edit tasks.
- Children can make their own tasks (auto assigned).
- Tasks created by adults can be assigned or left unassigned (They will go into the To-Do List). 
- Tasks can be flagged for important deadlines and private tasks can be limited to specific adults and children.
- Flagged or private tasks can only be edited or deleted by the creator.
- Children can make private tasks, but they are only private from other children.
- Adults can mark the task or event as a parent task, children can not be assigned or claim it
(We need to add something that prevents tasks being done early if they are a "time" or "day")

Pending (Adults):
- Tasks that are assigned to an adult, will come here for user appoval.
- If the task is flagged, an adult can still refuse the task, just the creator will be notified.
- Decined tasks go back into the To-Do List.

Parents:
- These are all the assigned tasks and etc assigned to your children.

Family: 
- This is a shared overview with everyone invited on your group, so everyone can see who’s doing what and when.
- If a task is private, it will not show up here.

To-Do:
- Unassigned tasks that can claimed or reassign.
- Adult tasks will not show up for children.

Medications:
- Dose reminders with notifications, acknowledgments (Taken / Remind Later / Reject for adults), follow-up reminders, and parent oversight for children.

Bills / Payments: Shared invoices, payment tracking, and optional bill splitting.

History:
A toggle-style view. One side shows a breakdown of all tasks assigned to you and their completion status, sortable by Complete, Incomplete, and task type. The other side shows medication history, likely as a statistical summary of completed doses and dates missed or rejected.
